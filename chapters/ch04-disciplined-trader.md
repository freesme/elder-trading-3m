# Chapter 4: 自律的交易者

## Core Idea

Discipline is not a personality slogan; it is an operating system built from rules, records, review, and position size. The trader's psychological weaknesses will appear in the market unless records expose them early.

## Frameworks Introduced

- **Self-sabotage detection**
  - When to use: after repeated losses, impulsive trades, or rule violations.
  - How: inspect the trading diary and equity curve. Repeated mistakes indicate a behavioral pattern, not bad luck.
- **Three-part training model**
  - When to use: designing a trader learning path.
  - How: increase responsibility gradually, score performance continuously, and rehearse actions until execution becomes automatic.
- **Record-driven discipline**
  - When to use: building self-control without an external manager.
  - How: maintain a trade spreadsheet, equity curve, diary, and plan; review them before increasing size.

## Key Concepts

- **Mind**: the discipline to act under uncertainty without chasing stimulation.
- **Excitement trading**: trading for adrenaline while pretending to trade for profit.
- **Self-awareness**: knowing the emotional motive behind the order.
- **Equity curve**: objective graph of account progress and behavioral quality.
- **Trade diary**: the narrative and visual record of why a trade was made and what was learned.

## Mental Models

- Use **market as test environment**: every trade grades your process whether you look at the score or not.
- Think of **small size as training wheels**: early trades are tuition; the goal is skill acquisition, not maximum profit.
- Use **external principle** when emotions rise: rules must stand outside the moment of temptation.

## Anti-patterns

- **Blaming brokers, experts, or news**: outsourcing responsibility for a decision you made.
- **Moving stops away from risk**: giving a losing trade more room after entry.
- **Avoiding records**: refusing to keep evidence because it would reduce the pleasure of impulsive trading.
- **Trying to skip apprenticeship**: expecting immediate large profits without gradual training and review.

## Key Takeaways

1. Trade only when the motive is profit under a plan.
2. Responsibility for every order stays with the trader.
3. Records are the main tool for converting painful losses into learning.
4. Small, repeated, reviewed practice is better than large emotional bets.
5. Discipline means doing the correct action, especially when the easy action is tempting.
