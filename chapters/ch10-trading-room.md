# Chapter 10: 走进我的交易室

## Core Idea

Completed trade logs demonstrate how Elder combines higher-timeframe context, daily divergences, Force Index, stops, targets, and record notes. The lesson is less about copying trades and more about copying the disciplined review structure.

## Frameworks Introduced

- **Completed-trade review**
  - When to use: studying examples without becoming attached to live positions.
  - How: review only closed trades to reduce ego and hindsight distortion.
- **Triple screen in practice**
  - When to use: mapping actual trade decisions.
  - How: long-term chart supplies strategic bias; daily chart supplies entry/exit clues; stops and targets are logged before and after.
- **Divergence cluster**
  - When to use: looking for high-quality reversals.
  - How: seek agreement among price, MACD histogram, MACD lines, Force Index, channel location, and support/resistance.
- **Trade log compression**
  - When to use: recording a trade efficiently.
  - How: brief notes on why the trade was found, why it was entered, where risk sat, and what triggered exit.

## Key Concepts

- **Strategic decision**: higher-timeframe direction or context.
- **Tactical adjustment**: shorter-timeframe entry and exit.
- **Bottom divergence**: lower price low with weaker downside indicator reading.
- **Top divergence**: higher price high with weaker upside indicator reading.
- **Channel capture**: profit measured as a portion of the relevant price channel.
- **Closed-trade objectivity**: easier to learn after the position no longer affects ego or capital.

## Mental Models

- Use **journal examples as templates, not signals**: copy the method of thinking, not the ticker.
- Think of **divergence cluster as evidence weight**: one clue is weak; multiple independent clues improve confidence.
- Use **exit notes as education**: exits often reveal more about discipline than entries.

## Anti-patterns

- **Case-copying**: imitating old tickers or patterns without current context.
- **Displaying live trades for validation**: inviting ego into unfinished decisions.
- **Verbose journaling avoidance**: thinking a journal needs essays; concise structured notes are sufficient.
- **Ignoring imperfect examples**: flawed trades are valuable because they show process gaps.

## Key Takeaways

1. Study completed trades slowly and critically.
2. Record entry, exit, and learning in a compact visual format.
3. Triple screen links strategic context to tactical execution.
4. Divergence is stronger when confirmed by multiple tools.
5. The next real task is building your own record system and risk plan.
