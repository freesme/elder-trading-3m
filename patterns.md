# Patterns

## 3M Trade Validation

**When to use**: Before taking any trade or reviewing a strategy.

**How**:
1. Mind: identify the emotional state and confirm the trade is not for stimulation, revenge, or rescue.
2. Method: state the setup, timeframe, entry trigger, stop, and target.
3. Money: calculate risk, position size, and current 6% exposure.
4. Trade only if all three are valid.

**Trade-offs**: This slows down execution, but prevents most impulsive trades.

## Activity Classification

**When to use**: When a position is being justified after entry.

**How**:
1. Ask whether the thesis is long-term value, short-term price swing, or excitement.
2. Check whether the exit rule matches that thesis.
3. If the position's new explanation differs from its original explanation, close or formally re-plan it.

**Trade-offs**: Forces uncomfortable exits, but prevents trade-to-investment drift.

## Triple Screen Setup

**When to use**: Building a swing or position trade.

**How**:
1. Choose a trading timeframe.
2. Move one timeframe higher for strategic direction.
3. Use trend-following tools on the higher timeframe.
4. Return to the trading timeframe and wait for a tactical pullback or oscillator signal.
5. Enter with a defined trigger.
6. Place stop and target before sizing.

**Trade-offs**: Fewer trades, better alignment; may miss fast reversals.

## Manual Bar-by-Bar System Test

**When to use**: Validating a discretionary or semi-systematic method.

**How**:
1. Load at least several years of history.
2. Hide future data.
3. Step one bar at a time.
4. Record every signal, order, stop, target, and adjustment.
5. Review results and decision quality.

**Trade-offs**: Slow, but teaches behavior that bulk backtests do not.

## 2% Position Sizing

**When to use**: Before every order.

**How**:
1. Account risk budget = trading equity x 0.02.
2. Per-unit risk = absolute(entry - stop) + estimated costs.
3. Quantity = floor(account risk budget / per-unit risk).
4. Reduce further for slippage and uncertainty.

**Trade-offs**: Rejects many attractive trades; preserves survival.

## 6% Monthly Risk Gate

**When to use**: Before adding risk and during drawdowns.

**How**:
1. At month start, record prior month-end equity.
2. Monthly risk budget = that value x 0.06.
3. Add realized losses and current open risk.
4. If total reaches the budget, stop opening risk and review.

**Trade-offs**: May force inactivity during a rebound; prevents emotional spirals.

## SafeZone Stop

**When to use**: Trailing stops in a trend.

**How**:
1. Measure recent adverse penetrations against the trend.
2. Average them over a chosen window.
3. Multiply by a factor.
4. Place the stop outside normal noise and trail only in the trade direction.

**Trade-offs**: Less obvious than support/resistance stops; needs calibration by market.

## Four-Record Review System

**When to use**: Setting up a trading operation.

**How**:
1. Spreadsheet: each trade, cost, P/L, scores.
2. Equity curve: monthly account value plus 2% and 6% columns.
3. Diary: annotated charts and emotions for selected trades.
4. Action plan: next-day instructions, prepared after close.

**Trade-offs**: Time-intensive; creates the feedback loop needed for improvement.

## ABC Watchlist Triage

**When to use**: Weekly market review.

**How**:
1. Review each watched instrument on weekly and daily templates.
2. Mark A if it can be traded tomorrow.
3. Mark B if it may be traded this week.
4. Mark C if no likely trade exists this week.
5. Spend most work on A, monitor B, ignore C.

**Trade-offs**: Can miss unexpected moves; greatly reduces attention waste.

## Action Plan Note

**When to use**: Preparing orders for the next session.

**How**:
1. Write weekly condition.
2. Write daily condition.
3. Write the exact action: entry, stop, target, size, and contingency.
4. Execute from the note, not memory.

**Trade-offs**: Reduces spontaneity; improves consistency.
